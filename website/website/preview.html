A migraine isn't a moment — it's a course of events

Pain builds and fades, symptoms come and go, and the medication you take unfolds over hours. Instead of asking you to summarise an attack after it's over, MigraLog lets you record what's happening as it happens and assembles it into a continuous timeline. That shape is what reveals patterns — and what a clinician can actually use.

HOME

Everything you need for today, on one screen

The home dashboard is built for a day in pain: big, obvious actions and nothing to fiddle with. Start an episode, log a dose, and see your recent history at a glance.

  • Today's medications — see scheduled doses, tap to log, or undo a mistake
  • One-tap Start Episode — begins tracking instantly; becomes Log Update while one is ongoing
  • Recent episodes — each with a sparkline showing how the attack's intensity rose and fell
EPISODES

Capture an attack as it unfolds

An episode records a single migraine from start to finish. Start it the moment one begins — or backdate it if you're catching up — and add to it as things change.

  • Where and when it started, and total duration — even across midnight
  • Pain intensity on a 0–10 scale, logged as often as it changes
  • Pain locations and symptoms, marked when they start and when they resolve
  • End it when it's over — and reopen it if you closed one by mistake
TIMELINE

One timeline for the whole attack

Every intensity update, symptom, location change, dose, and note shares a single chronological timeline — so you can see medication timing, symptom changes, and pain peaks all in relation to one another. It's essential when an attack spans many hours or even days.

  • Intensity trajectory shown as a sparkline across the whole episode
  • See exactly what you took and when, so you know when the next dose is due
  • Nothing is set in stone — press and hold any entry to fix a time or detail
MEDICATIONS

Log doses in a tap

Track rescue, preventative, and other medications with smart autocomplete for common migraine drugs. Quick-log your usual dose, or open the details to adjust quantity, backdate the time, and add a note.

  • Organised by type and class — Triptan, CGRP, NSAID, and more
  • Schedules for preventatives — daily, monthly, or quarterly — with reminders
  • Time-sensitive reminders, with optional critical follow-ups that alert even in Do Not Disturb
  • Doses logged during an attack attach to that episode automatically
SAFETY

Cooldowns and overuse limits that watch your back

Taking rescue medication too soon, or on too many days, is easy to lose track of when you're in pain. MigraLog keeps count and shows a clear warning — it never blocks you, it just helps you make an informed decision and gives you something concrete to raise with your doctor.

Per-medication cooldown

Set a minimum time between doses for a single medication. Its card shows how long ago the last dose was and how long remains — "Last dose 2h ago — wait 4h."

Per-class cooldown

Require a gap between any medications in the same class — useful for triptans. The warning names the drug that triggered it, even if you're about to log a different one.

Overuse safety limits

Track days of use against class guidelines over a rolling window. It turns amber as you approach a limit and red at or over it.

Suggested defaults follow common clinical guidance, but they're general starting points — not medical advice. Your neurologist's guidance always takes precedence.

CALENDAR

A month at a glance

Every day carries a colour, so over time you can see clusters of good days and difficult ones without reading a single entry. Migraine days are set automatically from your episodes — you only ever log the days in between.

  • Migraine day — automatic whenever an episode covers the day
  • Clear — a good day, no symptoms or concerns
  • Not Clear — prodrome, postdrome, or anxiety between attacks
  • Overlays — mark travel, illness, stress, or a cycle, and line life events up against your attacks
INSIGHTS

Your patterns, no extra work

Insights are built entirely from what you already log. Pick a range — 14, 30, 60, 90 days or custom — and see how often attacks happen, how severe they are, when they strike, and how your medications are working.

  • Migraine / clear / not-clear day counts and episode duration metrics
  • Headache burden, severity by week, time-of-day onset, and symptom & location frequency
  • Warning signs flagged against widely used clinical thresholds — chronic-range headache days, overuse risk, rising rescue use

Which rescue works — and how fast

The Med Response view ranks your rescue medications by how quickly they bring intensity down — the median time from a dose to the first drop in pain. Faster medications rise to the top, with a detail card for each.

Needs at least three doses with measurable relief before a medication is rated.
